I never expected it to be this nerve-wracking.

So many things can go wrong. I've been using the app for myself, but I can't even begin to imagine how others are going to use it. There are too many edge cases to consider — though intentionally, the application is meant to be used for your own convenience.

Creating a waitlist site has been as exciting as it has been nerve-wracking. Just having to build the security measures for the form input field has me nervous for what's to come in the future. The internet, for me, is a void. It's a place where anything can happen, and I'm not sure how people are going to take in this project I've been passionately working on.

## Built for myself first

I know people close to me are going to try to enjoy the product, but ultimately I made it for myself. I use it to track my own workout sessions — mainly strength and boxing related. Developing strength and developing this language for training has helped me a lot.

With boxing, I've even developed a language that my coach — who has way more experience — appreciates when we train. I love learning new things about fitness, about training, and teaching it to a computer so that it can help people train at scale.

So many people have misconceptions about what training is and what training should look like. I feel confident that my way of thinking can help them in some way. Or so I hope.

I'm optimistic about what can come from people using my application. But I also see where things can go wrong, and I'm trying my absolute best to tackle these issues as they come.

## The challenge of different gym-goers

One of the biggest issues I'm facing is providing the best experience for different types of gym-goers. I've only really done strength training, but I've seen the patterns of that training show up in other disciplines in the gym.

I did strength training because I stepped into a powerlifting gym when I was young, and that was the only type of training I was really able to learn growing up.

I got decent at it. People came up to me and asked how I was able to reach the numbers I reached. They even asked if I competed.

I had no idea what I was doing. Training was a black box for me, and the development of this application has helped me uncover that black box — and many black boxes that exist for digital systems.

## What building this has taught me

I was able to develop so many different systems across so many different layers for this application, and the lessons I've learned are priceless.

Each layer — from how progression rules work, to how sessions are tracked, to how a waitlist form is secured — has forced me to think about problems I never had to consider when I was just lifting for myself. The app started as a personal tool. Now it's becoming something other people will touch, and that shift changes everything.

I'm still figuring it out. But that's the point. The same beginner's mindset I bring to the gym — show up, learn, adjust — is the one I'm bringing to this launch.
